Homemade Old Bay Seasoning Recipe

The history of Old Bay seasoning for seafood is a tale as old as time. Created in the late 1800s, it has been used to prepare some of America's favorite dishes since its inception and continues to be popular today. When you think about your all-time favorite dish with shrimp or crab cakes on top, many people will say their go-to meal includes this delicious sauce that not only enhances flavor but also helps bring back memories full of childhood nostalgia.

Spicy, tasty, and actually kind of healthy.

Old Bay seasoning is the perfect way to spice up seafood dishes of all types. It not only tastes great, but it also contains a healthy amount of sodium and vitamin C!

Old bay seasoning is a staple in most kitchens because no dish can be boring when you add this unique ingredient from Maryland. The recipe for Old Bay's secret flavor comes down to three ingredients: celery salt, paprika powder, and ground mustard seed; making an already tasty meal even better with its extra kick that will leave your taste buds begging for more!

Old Bay seasoning is a spice mix with eight spices that can be used for seafood, soups, or sauces. It has been around since 1933 and was originally created in Maryland by two German immigrant brothers of the Bayer family when they were looking to create an all-purpose flavoring blend without salt as one of the ingredients during Prohibition

Old Bay Seasoning is a spice mixture made up of multiple different herbs/spices which are mostly commonly used on crustaceans like crabs and shrimp but also pairs well with other foods such as hamburgers. The recipe was first developed in Baltimore back during prohibition where it served more purposes than just its culinary uses (namely because Baltimore's water had high levels iron).

Flavor for your favorite seafoods

People love using old bay seasoning in their dishes because it has the perfect amount of spices and flavor. The best way to use it is on crabs, as well as other seafood like clams or shrimp.

Old Bay seasoning is a key ingredient in many dishes, and it's not just for seafood. Old Bay can be used as an all-purpose poultry rub or sprinkled on ribs before grilling.

Old bay seasoning was introduced to the American public back in 1945 by spice company McCormick & Co., but its roots are much older than that - sailors began using this blend of herbs and spices centuries ago when they started trading with Asia via colonial routes like Maryland where I grew up!

Old Bay seasoning is a great way to give your food a kick, and it's specifically designed for seafood dishes.

Old Bay seasoning has been around since the 1940s with its signature red color that represents old bay leaves being ground up into spices after months of drying. The herbs found in Old Bay spice are black pepper, cayenne pepper (for heat), paprika (to add more flavor along with adding some orangey tones) as well as celery salt among others!

It's Also Good on Burgers

Seasoning is an essential part of cooking, but some people like to experiment with different flavor combinations and spices. For example, you can add old bay seasoning on burgers for a more tangy taste or throw in salt and herbs if you're looking for something fresh tasting.

Old Bay seasoning is a popular American spice mixture that originated as many others have in the bay area of Baltimore, Maryland. It's often used on hamburgers to provide them with more flavor and taste.

Old Bay seasoning is a classic American spice that has become associated with hamburgers. Most famously, it can be found on the Wendy's Bacon Double Cheeseburger as well as their Spicy Chicken Sandwich, but if you're looking to try it in your home kitchen for grilling season then this easy recipe will guide you through how to do so!

Homemade Old Bay Seasoning Recipe

Dietary
Oil Free
Sugar Free
Low Carb
Calories per serving
8
Servings
16 (1/2 T per serving)
Ingredients

2 T bay leaf powder
1 T ground celery seed
1/2 T pink salt 
1 T dry mustard
2 t black pepper
2 t ground ginger
2 t smoked paprika
1 t ground nutmeg
1 t ground cloves
1 t ground allspice
1⁄2 t crushed red pepper flakes
1⁄2 t ground cardamom
1⁄4 t ground cinnamon

Directions
  1. Combine all ingredients together
  2. Store in airtight container
